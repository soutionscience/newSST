Access Control System

The basic objective of any Access Control solution is to protect an organization’s assets and ensure human safety by controlling movement of people to pre-defined rules.

Key Features.

Access Zone

Allows controls who (User) goes where (Access Zone) and when (Time) for each Access Zone.

Access Mode

Support for different access credentials (Finger, Palm Vein, RF Card, PIN).

Access Level

System can assign up to 15 different Access Level to users. Giving user different privileges.

First-In User Rule

In some organizations, employees or workers are not allowed to enter certain work areas unless a designated person enters first to ensure higher level of safety and security.

2-Person Rule

Is an access control feature that allows entry only when two persons from an authorized group jointly use their access credentials.

Man-Trap

The man trap access control feature dictates that second door should open only after the first has completely closed.

Guard Tour

Guard Tour facilitates enforcing patrol of security guards Dead-Man Zone.

Dead-Man Zone

makes it compulsory for the employee to confirm his presence at pre-defined interval.

Anti-Pass-Back

Anti-Pass-Back prevents a card holder from passing his card back to another person to gain unauthorized entry.

Do Not Disturb (DND) Zone

Do Not Disturb Zone feature is useful for meeting rooms or areas that should not be access because of specific events.

Biometric Systems

Biometric systems are access control systems that restrict or grant permission to a premises/ area after confirmation of an individual’s identity. This can be achieved through fingerprint, rectal and facial scans.
